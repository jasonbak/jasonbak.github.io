Professional info

Last summer, I was a legal software developer intern at Fulcrum GT in Chicago, Illinois building an engine to standardize and clean legal data to allow for applications to be more easily built upon it. My team and I used OWL and RDFS to create an ontology for our MarkLogic GraphDB, SPARQL to create dynamic queries, and ASP.NET MVC 5 Entity Framework to populate MarkLogic with a dynamic model of a law firm.

I am in my third-year at Carnegie Mellon University, where I TA CS 15-122: Principles of Imperative Programming, i.e., Data Structures, and study statistics/machine learning and computer science as an undergraduate student.
